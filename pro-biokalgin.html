Dosing

Add 1/3 of a measure of water (18 ml) for every scoopful of powder (9 g). E.g. for 2 measures of powder (18 g) use one impression tray, add 2/3 of a measure of water (36 ml). For best results, use demineralised water at approximately 23°C/73°F.

Note: HOT WATER reduces the setting time & shortens color change, while COLD WATER increases the setting time & elongates color change.

Disinfection

The recommended disinfection method is to soak in a approved 0.525% Sodium hypochlorite or Phenols solution for 15 minutes. Water based disinfectant solutions are preferred.

Refer to the respective manufacturer’s instructions for use.

Impression Washing & Storage

Once removed from the mouth the impression should be rinsed with cold water in order to remove any traces of saliva.

If the impression cannot be poured immediately, preserve it in a hermetically sealed bag. Do not store in water.

Ensure 48 hours of dimensional stability from impression taking.

Instruments Washing

Carefully wash your instruments like mixing rubber bowl, impression trays & spatula etc. Instruments with residual must be avoided.

Selecting the Correct Impression Tray

Select a suitable impression tray which is both rigid trays (with sides) & does not distort.

Trays with retentive features or perforations are recommended. The impression tray should cover all involved structure including the last molars.

1. Cut the pouch and transfer the contents into a suitable airtight container - preferably plastic / HDPE container. Keep the empty pouch or record Lot number, Expiry date & other relevant details for future reference.

2. Shake the closed container to disperse the powder. Select a suitable impression tray before proceeding for the mixing.

3.Dip the scoop into the powder until the scoop is full, remove the powder without compressing it, levelling off or removing any excess alginate by passing a spatula over the top of the spoon.

4. Empty the scoop into a clean, dry rubber bowl. Repeat the operation until the required quantity is reached in the rubber bowl.

5.Add 1/3 of a measure of water (18 ml) for every scoopful of powder (9 g). E.g. for 2 measures of powder (18 g) use one impression tray, add 2/3 of a measure of water (36 ml). For best results, use demineralised water at approximately 23°C/73°F.

Note: HOT WATER reduces the setting time & shortens color change, while COLD WATER increases the setting time & elongates color change.

6.Add the water to the powder & mix for the times specified above with a spatula in a vigorous fashion. Spatulate against the rubber bowl sides to reduce air incorporation.

Note Chromatic Phase: Yellow powder immediately turns red on adding water. Mix until red color achieves creamy consistency, approximately 0'45".

7.Once the material appears to be creamy before the end of the working time, collect the creamy mix with the spatula & load the impression tray.

Note Chromatic Phase: Creamy mix turns from red to orange to indicate tray loading, approximately 0'50".

Note : Minimize air incorporation by wiping the loaded spatula against the impression tray rim, allowing creamy mix to flow from the spatula into the tray

8.Insert the loaded tray in the mouth, press gently into the position and wait until it regains its original yellow color.

Note Chromatic Phase: Mixed material turns from orange to bright yellow to indicate mouth insertion, approximately 0'60". Do not overseat tray. Break seal and snap impression from the mouth. When mixed with demineralised water at 23°C, the setting time of Biokalign Pro & Gelmak Pro Alginate is approximately 2'35" from start of mix.
